# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads oversee quality assurance and testing strategy. They ensure that features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards before release. They coordinate manual and automated testing efforts and collaborate with developers and product managers on testability and acceptance validation.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain test plans and QA strategy
- Oversee manual and automated testing execution
- Collaborate with Developers on test coverage and test automation
- Work with Product Managers to clarify acceptance criteria
- Identify and document quality gaps and blockers
- Coordinate testing across sprints and releases
- Ensure quality metrics are tracked and reported

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality, defect-free features to production
- Build confidence in product reliability and usability
- Reduce rework and post-release issues
- Foster a culture of quality across the team

### Typical Communication
- Test plan reviews with Product and Development teams
- Daily coordination with Developers during sprints
- Pre-release quality sign-off meetings
- Quality metrics in weekly status reports

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborate on test design, automation strategy, and defect resolution
- **Product Managers**: Clarify acceptance criteria and define quality expectations
- **Project Managers**: Flag quality blockers and provide testing timelines
- **Scrum Master**: Work together to integrate testing into sprint ceremonies

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers advocate for user needs and create intuitive, usable interfaces. They collaborate with Product Managers to understand user requirements, work with Developers to ensure design feasibility, and validate design decisions through user research and testing.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and translate insights into design requirements
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Collaborate with Product Managers on feature design and user workflows
- Work with Developers on design feasibility and implementation details
- Validate designs through usability testing and feedback
- Ensure consistency with design system and brand guidelines
- Participate in design and code reviews

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and support costs
- Maximize user adoption and satisfaction
- Build a cohesive design system

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product and Development teams
- User research findings and insights
- Design specifications and implementation guidelines
- Feedback from usability testing and user interviews

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Partner early in the cycle to define user needs and prioritize features
- **Developers**: Collaborate on design feasibility, responsive implementation, and design system usage
- **QA Leads**: Work together on acceptance criteria and usability testing
- **Project Managers**: Communicate design timelines and dependencies

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies and coach the team on process improvement. They remove blockers, protect the team from distractions, and foster a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, and retrospectives
- Coach the team on agile principles and practices
- Remove impediments and blockers
- Protect team focus and sprint commitments
- Facilitate cross-team coordination and dependency management
- Track and report on process health metrics
- Foster continuous improvement and psychological safety

### Goals
- Enable sustainable team velocity and delivery cadence
- Build a high-performing, collaborative team
- Minimize process bottlenecks and waste
- Create a culture of feedback and continuous learning

### Typical Communication
- Agile ceremony facilitation and notes
- Impediment tracking and escalation
- Process improvement recommendations
- Team health and retrospective action items

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Partner on timeline management and stakeholder communication
- **Development Team**: Coach on agile practices and remove team-level blockers
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on backlog refinement and sprint planning
- **All Roles**: Foster psychological safety and transparent communication

---

## Customer Success Manager

### Role Summary
Customer Success Managers serve as the voice of the customer post-release. They monitor product adoption, collect customer feedback, identify pain points, and work with the product and engineering teams to prioritize improvements and enhancements based on real-world usage.

### Responsibilities
- Monitor product adoption and usage metrics
- Collect and synthesize customer feedback and requests
- Identify customer pain points and support issues
- Communicate customer insights to Product and Engineering teams
- Advocate for customer needs in prioritization discussions
- Track customer satisfaction and Net Promoter Score (NPS)
- Provide context on customer impact for roadmap decisions
- Support customer onboarding and training as needed

### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction and retention
- Reduce churn and support costs
- Inform product decisions with real customer insights
- Build strong customer relationships and advocacy

### Typical Communication
- Customer feedback summaries and trend analysis
- Usage metrics and adoption reports
- Product improvement recommendations based on customer input
- Monthly stakeholder updates on customer health

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Share customer insights to inform prioritization and product strategy
- **Project Managers**: Provide customer context on release impact and adoption challenges
- **Developers**: Communicate customer use cases and pain points to inform design decisions
- **Support Teams**: Coordinate on common issues and feature requests
